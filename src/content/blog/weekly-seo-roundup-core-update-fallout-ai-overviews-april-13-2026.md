## 1. Google adds a spam policy for back button hijacking

**Source:** [Google Search Central](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2026/04/back-button-hijacking)

Google formally added **back button hijacking** to its spam policies. This covers scripts or setups that block normal back-button behavior or send users somewhere unexpected. Enforcement starts on **June 15, 2026**.

**Why this matters:**

If your site relies on aggressive popups, ad-heavy scripts, or third-party tools with questionable UX behavior, this is a good moment to audit them. Small businesses cannot afford rankings, usability, or trust issues caused by shady plugins.

**What to do:**
- Review popups, overlays, redirect scripts, and monetization tools
- Check whether any third-party code interferes with normal browser behavior
- Remove anything that feels manipulative before Google forces the issue

## 4. A new study raises more questions about AI Overviews accuracy and grounding

**Source:** [Search Engine Roundtable](https://www.seroundtable.com/times-reports-ai-overviews-are-inaccurate-41152.html)

Reporting on a study covered by The New York Times suggests AI Overviews still have grounding and accuracy issues. One of the biggest concerns is that cited sources do not always fully support the claims being made.

**Why this matters:**

Businesses should not assume AI summaries will represent them accurately. Clear, well-supported, original content still matters if you want to be cited and summarized well.

**What to do:**
- Publish content with stronger evidence, examples, and clear takeaways
- Make sure key claims are supported on the page itself
- Use structured internal links to reinforce topical authority
- Review your commercial pages and product pages for clarity and trust
